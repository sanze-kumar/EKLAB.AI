Focus Areas

Structural Biology with AI

Protein structure prediction, mutational analysis, active-site mapping, and mechanism-focused studies on hostâ€“pathogen systems. We also support single-particle Cryo-EM and CryoET workflows, including preprocessing, reconstruction, and interpretation.

Data Science & AI for Biology

Using statistics, machine learning, and simple AI workflows to explore biological, clinical, and survey datasets in a transparent and reusable way. We also use GPT-style assistants to speed up analysis scripting and figure generation while keeping the workflow reproducible.

Tools: Python, R (ggplot2), MATLAB, scikit-learn, Jupyter/Quarto

Computational Biology

OMICS analysis, custom pipeline development, and integration of diverse data types to answer clear biological questions. AI-assisted tools help accelerate structure/function interpretation and automate key steps like single-cell annotation and variant calling.

Tools: ESM/ProtTrans, scvi-tools (scVI/scANVI), scGPT/CellTypist, DeepVariant, GPU workflows

Bioinformatics

Careful use of databases and tools for sequence analysis, annotation, target selection, and validation. We also use AI-enabled predictors to improve functional interpretation of proteins, domains, localization signals, and mutation impacts.

Tools: UniProt, NCBI/BLAST, InterPro, SignalP, DeepTMHMM, NetMHCpan/NetMHCIIpan, AlphaMissense

Computational Chemistry

Designing molecules and using physics-based simulations to understand how compounds behave, bind, and change over time. AI tools are used to speed up screening and prioritize candidates before heavier simulation workflows.

Tools: RDKit, DiffDock, EquiBind, GNINA, DeepChem, GROMACS

CADD

Computer-aided drug design pipelines to identify and refine potential molecules. AI methods support faster screening, smarter ranking, and improved hit-to-lead decisions, combined with structure-based and property-based filters.

Tools: AutoDock (GPU), Vina, GNINA, DiffDock, QSAR/ML models, ADMET predictors

Scientific Communication

Turning complex results into clear stories, methods, and figures that are reliable and easy to reproduce. AI-assisted writing and figure workflows help with clarity and consistency, while we keep the final output grounded in the data and references.

Tools: LaTeX/Overleaf, Illustrator/Inkscape, Python/R plotting, NotebookLM, Elicit
