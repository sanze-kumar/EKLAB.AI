About EKLAB.AI

EKLAB.AI is an independent research initiative focused on the intersection of biology, data, and artificial intelligence. The idea is simple: use modern computational tools to ask sharp scientific questions, build transparent workflows, and share practical, reproducible research with the community.

EKLAB.AI originally grew out of a personal project called Eklavya Research Lab. The new name keeps that spirit of self-driven learning (“Eklavya”) but in a shorter, modern form that reflects its core identity: an experimental lab built around code, models, and data. “EKLAB” echoes “Eklavya Lab”, and “.AI” highlights the focus on AI-guided discovery.

The work at EKLAB.AI spans structural biology, molecular modeling, simulation, and AI-assisted analysis. This includes things like protein–ligand modeling, molecular dynamics, cryo-EM/cryo-ET data interpretation, and machine-learning pipelines for drug discovery and biological systems. Wherever possible, tools, scripts, and protocols are designed to be open, modular, and easy to adapt.

EKLAB.AI is not a large institute or company. It is a small, flexible research space that collaborates with academic labs, students, and independent researchers. The goal is to show that serious, high-quality science can also come from compact, agile setups—powered by curiosity, careful methods, and smart use of computation.

Who we are

EKLAB.AI is a small, independent research lab built around curiosity, careful methods, and real-world problems. It is run by a Dr. Sanjay Kumar with experience in structural biology, computational chemistry, and AI-driven analysis, working with students, clinicians, and researchers on flexible, project-based collaborations.

Instead of a large institute, EKLAB.AI works like a focused studio: small teams, clear questions, transparent workflows, and results that others can reproduce, reuse, and build on.
